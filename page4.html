As we all know, Halloween always takes place on the last day of October, every October 31st. Although it seems like Halloween should only be 24 hours per year, thanks to time zones, any given date is actually taking place somewhere for 48 hours total. That's because the two time zones that separate the earliest time and latest time are exactly 24 hours apart. That means that Halloween lasts 24 hours in the earliest time zone, and as soon as it becomes November 1st, Halloween starts in the latest time zone, lasting an additional 24 hours, thus getting us to our total of 48 hours of Halloween per year. However, you may have realized that 48 hours per year is still far from always being Halloween, and you'd be right to think so. Allow me to explain, but to do so, we need to leave the atmosphere and go into outer space. As you may already know, when we see the sun, we aren't seeing the sun in its exact current form. We are actually looking into the past. Due to the distance between the sun and Earth and the speed of light, it actually takes about 8 minutes for the sun's light to reach Earth. This applies to all the stars we can see in they sky at night too. They are so far away, that the light from some of them take years to reach Earth. Some of them may no longer exist. And by using this logic, you can guess there from a certain distance away from Earth, your view of Earth would be in that 48 hour window in which Halloween takes place on Earth. However, the universe is always expanding, so as you go further and further away from Earth, there are an infinite amount of places in outer space where if you looked at Earth and were able to zoom in, you'd see Halloween taking place from previous years. Over a light year away, you would see last year's Halloween. A few more light years and you'd see Halloween 5 years ago. You can keep going further and further away until you can see Halloween from decades ago and earlier. So somewhere, a lot of “somewhere”s, in the universe, it is always Halloween.
